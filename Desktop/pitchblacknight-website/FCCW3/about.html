Our History

Founded in 1875. Still showing up.

First Congregational Church of Wilmette was founded in 1875, making it one of the oldest congregations on the North Shore. For 150 years, FCCW has been a gathering place for people who take both faith and justice seriously.

After World War II, FCCW played a leading role in resettling nearly 400,000 European refugees — one of the most significant humanitarian acts in the history of American Congregationalism. In the 1960s and 70s, the congregation was active in the civil rights and fair housing movements. During the Sanctuary Movement of the 1980s, FCCW committed to sheltering those seeking safety.

Today, FCCW is home to Gates Manor, affordable senior housing on our grounds — a living example of faith and community care working together.

What We Believe

An Open & Affirming faith.

FCCW is an Open & Affirming congregation of the United Church of Christ — which means every person is truly welcome, regardless of background, identity, sexuality, or where they are on life's journey.

We believe in the priesthood of all believers — that each person has direct access to God and the responsibility to follow their conscience. We hold scripture seriously, interpreted through reason, experience, and the ongoing work of the Holy Spirit in the life of the community.

We hold our tradition with open hands and our neighbors with open hearts.

"We seek to follow the way of Jesus — not as the only path, but as the path we know best."

— FCCW Statement of Faith

Our Denomination

Part of the United Church of Christ

FCCW is a congregation of the United Church of Christ — a denomination with a long history of being on the right side of history. The UCC was the first mainline denomination to ordain an openly gay minister (1972), the first to affirm same-sex marriage (2005), and has consistently advocated for racial justice, environmental stewardship, and economic equity.

"God is still speaking" — the UCC's affirmation that faith is ongoing, not fixed. We believe it too.
