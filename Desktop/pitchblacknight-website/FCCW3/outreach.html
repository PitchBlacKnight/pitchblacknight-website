Service & Justice

Outreach

We don't just talk about a better world. We work toward one — in Wilmette, on the North Shore, and far beyond.

Our Commitment

"Faith without works is dead."

At FCCW, outreach isn't a committee — it's a way of being. From helping the unhoused in Evanston to an interfaith partnership with our Jewish neighbors, we show up for people because that's what we believe faith calls us to do.

Community Partner

Helping the Unhoused

FCCW partners with Connections for the Homeless in Evanston to serve our neighbors living without stable housing. Our members volunteer at the shelter and help prepare and serve meals — showing up in a tangible way for people in their most vulnerable moments.

No special skills needed. Just a willingness to show up. Volunteer opportunities are open to everyone.

Interfaith Partner

Congregation Sukkat Shalom

FCCW has a longstanding partnership with Congregation Sukkat Shalom, a progressive Jewish community in Wilmette. We share a commitment to open, inclusive faith and a vision of a more just world.

Our relationship is built on genuine dialogue, shared learning, and showing up for one another's community life. Understanding begins with relationship — and relationship begins with showing up.

More Ways We Serve

Local & global. Always showing up.

Our outreach extends in many directions — because need shows up in many forms.

🏫
Wilmette Community Nursery School
Serving North Shore families with early childhood education for decades. Open to all faiths and backgrounds, housed in our building, deeply connected to our mission of welcome.
🌿
Earth Care Ministry
Caring for creation is an act of justice. We work to reduce our building's footprint, educate our congregation, and advocate for environmental policies that protect the vulnerable.
🕊️
Interfaith Dialogue
We build relationships with Jewish, Muslim, Hindu, and other faith communities in the area — because understanding and solidarity begin with showing up and listening.
✊
Racial Justice
Anti-racism is ongoing work. Our Racial Justice Team leads education, facilitated conversations, and action — inside our congregation and in the broader community.
🍽️
Community Meals
Partnering with local food programs to ensure no one in Wilmette goes hungry. Volunteers help prepare and serve meals on a rotating schedule throughout the year.
📦
Holiday Giving Drives
Thanksgiving food baskets, Christmas toy collections, school supply drives — tangible ways to make life a little better for families in need right in our backyard.
