For First-Time Visitors

What to expect on Sunday.

We want you to feel at ease. Here's what a typical Sunday at FCCW looks like — no surprises, no pressure.

1
Arrive & be welcomed
Greeters meet you at the door with a bulletin and a genuine hello. The sanctuary opens about 20 minutes before service. Come as you are — jeans, dress, whatever feels right.
2
Children get settled
Children through 5th grade start in the sanctuary and are dismissed to Sunday School after the first hymn. Nursery care for infants and toddlers is available the whole time.
3
Worship together
About 60–75 minutes of music, prayer, scripture, and a sermon. Our worship is rooted in the UCC tradition — open, thoughtful, and never boring. Communion is celebrated monthly.
4
Stay for coffee hour
After service, everyone gathers in Fellowship Hall for coffee, snacks, and conversation. This is where the community really happens — and you'll be introduced, not ignored.

Common Questions

Do I need to be a member?
Not at all. You're welcome to attend as long as you like with no obligation to join.
Is FCCW affirming of LGBTQ+ people?
Yes — fully and joyfully. We are an Open & Affirming congregation. Everyone is welcome at every table.
What if I'm not sure what I believe?
You're in good company. Questions and doubts are not obstacles to faith here — they're part of it.
What's the dress code?
There isn't one. Come in whatever you'd wear on a relaxed Sunday morning.
Can I watch online?
Yes! Worship is livestreamed every Sunday and recordings are available on our YouTube channel.

Throughout the Year

Special Services & Seasons

Our worship year follows the rhythms of the church calendar, with special services that mark the sacred seasons.

Advent & Christmas Eve
The season of waiting and wonder. Candlelight Christmas Eve worship is one of our most beloved annual gatherings — all are welcome.
Easter & Holy Week
From Palm Sunday through the resurrection — a full week of services marking the central story of our faith.
All Saints Remembrance
A service of remembrance for those we love who have died. Candles lit, names read, grief honored in community.
Interfaith Blessing of the Animals
Bring your pets for a blessing in our tradition of honoring all of creation. A beloved autumn tradition open to the whole community.
Sound Bath & Contemplative Prayer
Spiritual enrichment beyond Sunday — sound bath meditation, wisdom walks in nature, and contemplative practices offered throughout the year.
Study Series
Seasonal study series exploring scripture, theology, and current events through a faith lens. Open to all — members and guests alike.
